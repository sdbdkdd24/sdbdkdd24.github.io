14:00 – 14:30 Keynote Talk 1: Onchain Data Analytics: Overview & Case Studies
Prof. Feida ZHU, Associate Professor and the Associate Dean of Singapore Management University, Singapore
Abstract: In the rapidly evolving landscape of blockchain and decentralized finance (DeFi), the analysis of onchain data has emerged as a critical tool...
Abstract: In the rapidly evolving landscape of blockchain and decentralized finance (DeFi), the analysis of onchain data has emerged as a critical tool for understanding and optimizing digital ecosystems. This talk will provide an overview of onchain data analytics, highlighting its significance in driving transparency, security, and efficiency within decentralized networks. The talk will explore how onchain data analytics sheds insight into the decentralization of token distribution from an asset perspective. By examining case studies, we will delve into practical applications where onchain analytics has played a pivotal role in early fraud detection, identifying malicious activities before they can compromise the integrity of the network. Additionally, we will discuss how intention analysis, powered by onchain data, can provide valuable insights into user behavior, aiding in the development of more secure and user-centric blockchain applications.

14:30 – 15:00 Keynote Talk 2: Building Digital Civilization: putting AI in a box
Dr. Bangdao CHEN, University of Oxford, United Kingdom
Abstract: The advance of digital technology brings significant benefits to humanity. However, there is a growing demand for greater transparency and visibility...
Abstract: The advance of digital technology brings significant benefits to humanity. However, there is a growing demand for greater transparency and visibility of risks before integrating more digital technologies into daily life and delegating critical functions to AI-driven services. Convincing existing tech companies, teams, or individuals to prioritise security and regulation is unrealistic without clear benefits. One solution is to create a well-regulated but decentralised system that runs parallel to what we might consider the "free-style" digital world. It offers a new direction and ecosystem that encourages and motivates the development of regulations, transparency, and the prevention of potential risks from rogue AI or systems.

Bio: Dr. Bangdao Chen received his Bachelor degree of Computer Science from Shanghai Jiaotong University, and both his Master’s and PhD in Computer Science from University of Oxford, where he also served as postdoctoral researcher. His main research directions include decentralised identity, payment security and communication security. He currently works on research and product development of information security, blockchain and other related technology. Together with his supervisor, Professor Bill Roscoe, he co-founded The Blockhouse Technology Ltd., Oxford University College Blockchain Research Center, Oxford Hainan Blockchain Research Institute and other enterprises and research institutions.

Panel discussion list:
1. How can AI enhance the scalability, security, and efficiency of blockchain and Web3 applications, particularly in creating a sustainable digital economy? Or the other way around, how can Web3 benefit AI for the same goal? Can you provide examples of successful implementations or ongoing projects that illustrate this integration?
2. What role do blockchain and Web3 technologies play in fostering a more sustainable and ethically responsible digital economy? How can AI contribute to addressing challenges such as energy consumption, data privacy, and fair distribution of resources within these ecosystems?
3. How can collaboration between academia and industry drive innovation in the development of AI-powered blockchain and Web3 solutions? What are the key challenges and opportunities in translating academic research into real-world applications that support a sustainable digital economy?
4. What are the emerging trends at the intersection of AI, blockchain, and Web3 that will shape the future of the digital economy? How can academic institutions and industry leaders work together to equip the next generation of professionals with the necessary skills to thrive in this evolving landscape?
